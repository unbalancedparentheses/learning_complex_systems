# Learning Complex Systems

Emergence, nonlinear dynamics, power laws, networks, and agent-based modeling.

## Books

### Introductory
- [Complexity: A Guided Tour](https://www.amazon.com/Complexity-Guided-Tour-Melanie-Mitchell/dp/0199798109) - Melanie Mitchell. Best general introduction. Covers all the key ideas accessibly.
- [Thinking in Systems](https://www.amazon.com/Thinking-Systems-Donella-H-Meadows/dp/1603580557) - Donella Meadows. Systems thinking for everyone. Short and brilliant.
- [Sync: How Order Emerges from Chaos](https://www.amazon.com/Sync-Order-Emerges-Universe-Nature/dp/0786887214) - Steven Strogatz. Synchronization across biology, physics, and society.

### Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos
- [Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos](https://www.amazon.com/Nonlinear-Dynamics-Chaos-Applications-Nonlinearity/dp/0738204536) - Steven Strogatz. The standard textbook. Beautifully written with great exercises.
- [Chaos: Making a New Science](https://www.amazon.com/Chaos-Making-Science-James-Gleick/dp/0143113453) - James Gleick. The history and ideas of chaos theory for a general audience.
- [Dynamics and Bifurcations](https://www.amazon.com/Dynamics-Bifurcations-Texts-Applied-Mathematics/dp/0387971416) - Hale & Koçak. More rigorous treatment.

### Networks
- [Linked: How Everything Is Connected](https://www.amazon.com/Linked-Everything-Connected-Business-Everyday/dp/0465085733) - Albert-László Barabási. Scale-free networks for a general audience.
- [Networks, Crowds, and Markets](https://www.cs.cornell.edu/home/kleinber/networks-book/) - Easley & Kleinberg. Free online. Bridges networks, game theory, and markets.
- [Network Science](http://networksciencebook.com/) - Barabási. Free online. The textbook.

### Power Laws and Fat Tails
- [Why Stock Markets Crash](https://www.amazon.com/Why-Stock-Markets-Crash-Financial/dp/0691175950) - Didier Sornette. Critical phenomena in financial markets.
- [Critical Mass](https://www.amazon.com/Critical-Mass-Thing-Leads-Another/dp/0374530416) - Philip Ball. Physics of society.
- [The Black Swan](https://www.amazon.com/Black-Swan-Improbable-Robustness-Fragility/dp/081297381X) - Nassim Taleb. Extreme events and our failure to predict them.
- [Scale](https://www.amazon.com/Scale-Universal-Innovation-Sustainability-Organisms/dp/0143110906) - Geoffrey West. Scaling laws in biology, cities, and companies.

### Agent-Based Modeling
- [Growing Artificial Societies](https://www.amazon.com/Growing-Artificial-Societies-Science-Adaptive/dp/0262550253) - Epstein & Axtell. The Sugarscape model and bottom-up social science.
- [Agent-Based and Individual-Based Modeling](https://www.amazon.com/Agent-Based-Individual-Based-Modeling-Practical-Introduction/dp/0691190836) - Railsback & Grimm. Practical introduction with NetLogo.

### Deep Dives
- [Dynamics of Complex Systems](https://www.amazon.com/Dynamics-Complex-Systems-Studies-Nonlinearity/dp/0813341213) - Yaneer Bar-Yam. Free online. Comprehensive and rigorous.
- [At Home in the Universe](https://www.amazon.com/At-Home-Universe-Self-Organization-Complexity/dp/0195111303) - Stuart Kauffman. Self-organization and the origin of order in biology.
- [The Origins of Order](https://www.amazon.com/Origins-Order-Self-Organization-Selection-Evolution/dp/0195079515) - Stuart Kauffman. The technical version. Dense but rewarding.
- [Information, Physics, and Computation](https://www.amazon.com/Information-Physics-Computation-Statistical-Monographs/dp/0198570836) - Mézard & Montanari. Statistical physics meets CS.

## Papers

### Foundational
- [More is Different](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.177.4047.393) - P.W. Anderson (1972). Emergence and why reductionism has limits.
- [Self-Organized Criticality](https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.59.381) - Bak, Tang, Wiesenfeld (1987). Sandpiles and power laws.
- [Collective Dynamics of Small-World Networks](https://www.nature.com/articles/30918) - Watts & Strogatz (1998). Small-world networks.
- [Emergence of Scaling in Random Networks](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.286.5439.509) - Barabási & Albert (1999). Preferential attachment and scale-free networks.

### Applications
- [Flocking: A Simple Model](https://www.red3d.com/cwr/boids/) - Craig Reynolds. Emergent flocking from simple rules.
- [An Introduction to Agent-Based Modeling](https://www.jstor.org/stable/24966421) - Wilensky & Rand. Survey of the field.
- [Power Laws, Pareto Distributions and Zipf's Law](https://arxiv.org/abs/cond-mat/0412004) - Newman (2005). When are power laws real?
- [Catastrophic Cascade of Failures in Interdependent Networks](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature08932) - Buldyrev et al. (2010). Cascading failures.

### Santa Fe Institute
- [Santa Fe Institute Working Papers](https://www.santafe.edu/research/results/working-papers) - Ongoing research from the home of complexity science.
- [Complexity Explorer](https://www.complexityexplorer.org/) - Free courses from SFI.

- [Santa Fe Institute: Nonlinear Dynamics](https://www.complexityexplorer.org/courses/166-nonlinear-dynamics-mathematical-and-computational-approaches) - Free.

- [Stanford CS224W: Machine Learning with Graphs](http://web.stanford.edu/class/cs224w/) - Network analysis and graph neural networks.

## Connections

- **Economics**: Market crashes, herding behavior, complex adaptive markets
- **Biology**: Ecosystems, evolution, gene regulatory networks
- **Fat tails**: Power laws, extreme events, self-organized criticality
- **Distributed systems**: Emergent behavior, cascading failures, network resilience
